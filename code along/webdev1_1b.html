Filipino Kare-Kare

Kare-kare is a Philippine stew that features a thick savory peanut sauce. It is generally made from a base of stewed oxtail, beef tripe, pork hocks, calves' feet, pig's feet or trotters, various cuts of pork, beef stew meat, and occasionally offal.



Ingredients

For the Kare-Kare:

  • 1 pound oxtail, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1.5 pounds tripe, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 large eggplant, sliced
  • 1 bunch of string beans, cut into 2-inch lengths
  • 1 bunch of pechay, leaves separated
  • 1 medium-sized banana blossom, sliced
  • 1/2 cup of achuete seeds, soaked in 1/2 cup of water, and strained (for coloring)
  • 1 medium-sized onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons of cooking oil
  • 8 cups of water
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Kare-Kare Sauce:

  • 1 cup of peanut butter
  • 2 cups of rice washed water (water used to rinse rice)
  • 1/2 cup of ground toasted rice
  • Fish sauce or salt, to taste


Steps

  1. In a large pot, boil the oxtail and tripe in 8 cups of water until tender for 2-3 hours.
  2. Skim off any scum that rises to the surface during boiling. Add more water if necessary to maintain the water level.
  3. Blanch the eggplant, string beans, pechay, and banana blossom in boiling water until they are slightly tender. Drain and set aside.
  4. In a separate pan, heat the cooking oil over medium heat. Saute the garlic and onions until fragrant and translucent.
  5. Add the peanut butter and achuete water to the pan. Stir well until the peanut butter is fully incorporated.
  6. Pour in the rice washed water and ground toasted rice. Stir until the sauce thickens.
  7. Season with fish sauce or salt to taste. Adjust the thickness and seasoning according to your preference.
  8. Once the meat is tender, add the prepared vegetables to the pot with the meat.
  9. Pour the Kare-Kare sauce over the meat and vegetables.
  10. Simmer for another 5-10 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld and the vegetables to fully cook.
  11. Transfer the Kare-Kare to a serving platter and serve with bagoong on the side.

Enjoy your homemade Kare-Kare, a delicious Filipino dish! Note: Serves 2-3 people only
